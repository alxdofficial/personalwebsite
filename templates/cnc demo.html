Make Something out of Nothing
Visual Design
My joint capability to model, 3D print, and CNC gives me the power of customization. I’ve designed and manufactured various ornamental pieces, as well as functional components.
Prototypes are Tangible Feelings
I use SLA and FDM 3D printing to process plastics, and a 3 axis CNC to mill aluminum. Jewelry usually needs lost wax casting, which is outsourced when needed. But with resin printing, I’m able to have something to hold even in the design phase.
More Remaking than Making
A lot can go wrong making something for the first time. End-Mills break, resin supports warp, and filaments delaminate. The experience of a machinist is the ability to diagnose issues quickly and iterate to the next revision.
Precision Powers Team Collaboration
This is a foldable 3D printer frame from my university’s design team. The task of making sub-components of the design was distributed to many team members, and everyone needs to return a part within tight tolerances for assembly to be possible.
